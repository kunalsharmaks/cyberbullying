Q. What is cyber bullying?


Cyber bullying is primarily psychological as its perpetrated through social manipulation and intimidation via messaging and interference with one’s social status through message boards and groups.


Q. Why does cyber bullying still exists?


Probably for much the same reason that people believe any form of abuse doesn’t exist—denial. It’s easier to say that the victim should “grow a thicker skin” or “stop being so defensive” than to admit that someone is being abusive and the victim has every right to be offended. Many people still think that verbal/written bullying is only about racial slurs, harassment of someone with a physical/mental disability, threats of physical harm, and other older-fashioned methods, like those utilized by elementary schoolers. However, most verbal abuse today is much more mainstream, taking the form of someone “expressing their thoughts/feelings” in such a way as to tear down someone else.


Q. What is the purpose of cyber-crime portals?


This portal is an initiative to facilitate victims/complainants to report cyber-crime complaints online. At present this portal caters to complaints pertaining to online stacking, harassment, sexual abuse material or sexually explicit content. The portal also provides an option of reporting an anonymous complaint, where complainants are not required to disclose their identity.


Q. What actions will be taken if complaints reports any false complaint/information?


False information provided by complainant may lead to penal action as per law.
